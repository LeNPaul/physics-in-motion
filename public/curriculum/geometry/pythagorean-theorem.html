Pythagorean Theorem

For a right angled triangle, the following relations apply:

The Pythagorean theorem is defined by \(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\), where \(a\) is the opposite side from a non-right angle of the right triangle, \(b\) is the adjacent side, and \(c\) is the hypotenuse, the longest side of a right triangle.

The trigonometric functions sine, cosine, and tangent are deinfed as follows:

$$\sin \theta = \frac{a}{c}$$

$$\cos \theta = \frac{b}{c}$$

$$\tan \theta = \frac{a}{b}$$

From the above definitions and the Pythagorean theorem, it follows that:

$$\sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1$$

$$\tan\theta = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$$

It is useful to measure angles in radians, rather than degrees, where \(2\pi \ rad = 360^\circ\)
