Scientific Notation

Very large and very small numbers are most conveniently expressed in scientific notation. Examples of what scientific notation looks like can be seen below.

\( 10^3 = 10 \times 10 \times 10 \)

\( 10^5 = 100,000 \)

\( 10^{-2} = \frac{1}{100} = 0.01 \)

\( 10^{-5} = \frac{1}{100,000} = 0.00001 \)

\( 275,000 = 2.75 \times 100,000 = 2.75 \times 10^{5} \)

\( 0.0032 = 3.2 \times 0.001 = 3.2 \times 10^{-3} \)

The most important rule to remmber is that when powers of \(10\) are multipled, the exponents are added, and when they are divided, the exponents are subtracted:

\( 10^m \times 10^n = 10^{m + n} \)

\( \frac{10^m}{10^n} = 10^{m - n} \)
