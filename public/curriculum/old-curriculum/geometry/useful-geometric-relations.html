Useful Geometric Relations

The following are some useful geometric relations:

Area of triangle of base \(b\) and height \(h\): \( \frac{1}{2} b h \)

Circumference of circle of radius \(r\): \( C = 2 \pi r \)

Area of circle of radius \(r\): \( A = \pi r^2 \)

Volume of sphere of radius \(r\): \( V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \)

Surface area of sphere of radius \(r\): \( A = 4 \pi r^2 \)

Volume of cylinder of radius \(r\) and height \(h\): \( V = \pi r^2 h \)

When two straight lines intersect, the opposite angles formed are equal. Two angles are equal when the lines forming them are parallel, or when the lines are mutally perpendicular.

The sum of the angles of a triangle is \(180^\circ\). A right triangle has one angle that is \(90^\circ\). An isosceles has two equal angles. An equilateral triangle has three equal sides, each one being \(60^\circ\).

Two triangles are similar if two of their angles are equal. The sides of two similar triangles will be proportional to each other, that is:

$$\frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2} = \frac{c_1}{c_2}$$
